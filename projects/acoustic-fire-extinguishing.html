Acoustic Fire Extinguishing System

Study and implementation of an acoustic fire suppression prototype based on low-frequency sound waves, combining physics modeling, analog electronics, and experimental validation.
Analog Electronics Acoustics Wien Bridge Oscillator LM386 Prototype Design

Overview

This project investigates the use of low-frequency acoustic waves to disturb and extinguish flames. The work included theoretical study of sound propagation, simulation of the extinction mechanism, design of a low-frequency generator, implementation of an audio amplification stage, and prototype testing.

Main Objective

Design an acoustic extinguisher capable of generating and amplifying low-frequency sound waves to experimentally evaluate their effect on flame disturbance and extinction.

System Architecture

  • Low-frequency generator based on a Wien bridge oscillator
  • Audio amplifier using LM386
  • 80 W / 6 Ω loudspeaker
  • Acoustic collimator to improve sound focusing

Technical Work

  • Study of sound wave propagation in air
  • Simulation of flame perturbation mechanism
  • Frequency range investigation
  • Oscillator design and implementation
  • Audio amplification and prototype assembly

Key Result

Experimental tests showed an effective flame extinction range approximately between 40 Hz and 85 Hz, with improved efficiency when using an acoustic collimator.

Skills Developed

  • Analog electronics design
  • Oscillator and amplifier design
  • Experimental prototyping
  • Scientific modeling
  • System-level validation
