Acoustic Fire
Extinguishing

Design and experimental validation of a fire suppression system based on low-frequency acoustic waves combining physics, analog electronics and prototyping.

Overview

This project explores how sound waves can disrupt flame stability. By generating controlled low-frequency acoustic waves and amplifying them, it is possible to destabilize combustion and extinguish fire without chemicals.

System Design

  • Wien bridge oscillator → low-frequency generation
  • LM386 amplifier → power amplification
  • 80W loudspeaker → acoustic emission
  • Collimator → directional sound focusing

Key Result

Effective extinction observed in the range 40 Hz – 85 Hz. Performance significantly improves when acoustic energy is focused.
