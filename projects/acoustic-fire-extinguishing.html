Physics / Electronics

Acoustic Fire
Extinguishing

Study and implementation of a fire suppression prototype based on low-frequency acoustic waves, combining wave physics, analog electronics, experimental testing, and presentation material.

Acoustics Analog Electronics Wien Bridge Oscillator LM386 Prototype Validation

Key takeaway

The most effective extinction range was approximately 40 Hz – 85 Hz, with improved performance when using a collimator to focus the acoustic energy.

Overview

This project explores how low-frequency sound waves can destabilize a flame and contribute to fire extinction without relying on chemical agents.

The work included theoretical analysis of acoustic wave propagation, experimental investigation of the useful frequency range, design of a Wien bridge oscillator, implementation of an LM386-based amplification stage, and assembly of a complete prototype.

Project Details

Problem Statement

How can sound waves be used to extinguish fire, and how can an acoustic extinguisher be designed and implemented?

Key Result

Effective extinction was observed in the range 40 Hz – 85 Hz, with better results when the acoustic energy was focused.

System Design

  • Wien bridge oscillator for low-frequency generation
  • LM386 audio amplifier for signal amplification
  • 80 W / 6 Ω loudspeaker for acoustic emission
  • Collimator for directional sound focusing

Skills Developed

  • Analog electronics design
  • Oscillator and amplifier implementation
  • Experimental prototyping
  • Scientific modeling
  • System-level validation
